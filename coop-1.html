Co-op Work Term

1

Keypath Education - Fall 2021

For my first co-op position, I worked as a frontend developer at Keypath Education. Keypath is a company that develops interactive elements for university courses. Basically, they develop anything more complicated than simple text on a page that you might see in an online university course. This can range from a click-to-reveal course element, to a practice quiz to test a student's knowledge or even a poll that returns that data to the faculty using a database.

1

Technical

When I had started at Keypath I knew that my technical abilities would be tested. I hadn't had much experience in web technologies up until that point, with just one website project under my belt. Through working as a frontend developer, I gained important knowledge in HTML5, CSS3, and vanilla JavaScript, as well as JQuery. I also made a few interactive pieces that used a database component in order to log comments and responses from users. I feel like I got a good sampling of what skills are required for a frontend developer position, having experienced both the design and the functional side of making the front end of a website.

2

Accessibility

Accessibility is an aspect of design that I never had to consider up until starting this position in September 2021. As Keypath is involved in education, it is imperative for all of their work to be accessible for all users. As such, I had to consider multiple forms of input and output, including keyboard navigation, screen reader usage and colour contrast. I strived to make all my pieces at least AA WCAG compliant, or AAA if possible, which was always verified by my supervisors who were experts when it comes to accessibility. If you have the means to test accessibility compliance yourself, you can test this page, which is keyboard navigable and screen reader ready.

3

Workflow and Organization

Throughout my time at Keypath, I had to use various Atlassian work related softwares in order to track my time and stay organized. This ranged from Jira to manage tasks, to Tempo which helped track my time. This was also my first time using git in a professional setting, as well as using it alongside other people. I learned how to make and review pull requests, and resolve merge conflicts when they came up.

4

Teamwork

In all of my academic career up until this point, I hadn't once had to collaborate with another person on code. However, as I was a member of the development team at Keypath, collaboration was impossible to avoid. I learned how to submit my own code in the form of a pull request, as well as review other people's pull requests. On top of that, regular meetings and code presentations ensured that I would constantly interact with other team members, and have a chance to share my insight, as well as receive recommendations from them.
